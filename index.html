AUTUMN IN THE PARK

Mammals

Killarney National Park in Ireland is home to diverse mammalian species. Red deer, the park's emblematic mammal, roam freely. Foxes, badgers, and pine martens thrive in the woodlands.

"The majestic creatures within the park

Killarney National Park is home to a rich array of animals, from the elusive red deer, Ireland's native deer species, to a variety of birdlife. The park's woodlands and meadows provide a natural haven for these animals, allowing visitors to witness the captivating wildlife in a picturesque setting.

Birds

Rare Flora

Killarney National Park shelters rare flora, including the elusive Killarney fern and orchids, enhancing the park's ecological significance and beauty.

Magic in the Roots

In Killarney National Park, a vibrant tapestry of flora unfolds, featuring native species such as the delicate purple orchids and the iconic rhododendrons. The lush woodlands and meadows showcase a harmonious blend of wildflowers, creating a botanical haven that enchants visitors with its natural beauty.

Vegetation

Killarney National Park's diverse vegetation encompasses ancient oak woodlands, vibrant heathlands, and lush wetlands. Orchids, ferns, and rare plants thrive, creating a rich tapestry of flora within this Irish gem.
